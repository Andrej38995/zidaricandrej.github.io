Bitcoin Logarithmic Regression Bands
Bitcoin logarithmic regression bands show a blue BTC/USD values, reflecting the most recent chart update.
Additionally, there are two upper regression bands depicted in red and three lower regression bands in green.
These bands are derived from a dataset that distinguishes between bubble and non-bubble territories.
The upper band associated with a bubble (BUB) and the lower band corresponding to a bubble (BLB) indicate an extremely optimistic region for Bitcoin's price.
They signify the tops of market cycles.

On the other hand, the upper band for non-bubble values (NBU), the fitted non-bubble band (NBF), and the lower band for non-bubble values (NBL),
represent logarithmic regression values obtained by excluding market cycle euphoria. These bands represent price ranges associated with non-bubble conditions.

In the historical context, when the Bitcoin price approaches the bright green NBL band, it has typically indicated a potential market cycle bottom.
Conversely, if the price nears the bright red BUB band, it suggests a possible market cycle top.
