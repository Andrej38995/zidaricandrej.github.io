Bitcoin Dominance
Bitcoin dominance refers to the market capitalization and influence of Bitcoin (BTC) relative to other cryptocurrencies.
It is a measure of Bitcoin's share of the total market value of all cryptocurrencies combined.

Bitcoin dominance is calculated by dividing the market capitalization of Bitcoin by the total market capitalization of all cryptocurrencies and then multiplying by 100 to obtain a percentage.
The dominance percentage represents the proportion of the cryptocurrency market that is accounted for by Bitcoin.
Bitcoin Dominance Excluding Stablecoins

Bitcoin dominance excluding stablecoins refers to the calculation of Bitcoin's market dominance while excluding the market capitalization of stablecoins.
The exclusion of stablecoins can provide a different perspective on Bitcoin's market share within the cryptocurrency ecosystem.
By excluding stablecoins from the calculation, the focus is shifted to the market dominance of Bitcoin relative to other cryptocurrencies.
It allows for a clearer understanding of the relative strength and influence of Bitcoin within the broader cryptocurrency landscape.
